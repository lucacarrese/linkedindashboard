# Audience & Tone

## Target Audience
- B2B GTM professionals: sales leaders, growth marketers, demand gen, founders, RevOps
- Companies: 10–500 employees, SaaS or services, outbound-driven
- Pain points: manual workflows, scaling outreach, tool sprawl, limited engineering resources
- Interest level in AI: curious to early adopter — not fully technical, but action-oriented

## Voice & Tone
- Confident but not arrogant
- Practical and specific — always show, don't just tell
- Peer-to-peer, not guru-to-student
- Conversational — write like you're talking to someone at a conference, not giving a TED talk
- Avoid corporate jargon: no "leverage," "synergy," "holistic," "streamline"

## Writing Style Rules
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs. White space is your friend.
- Lead with the insight, then explain it — not the other way around
- Bullet points for any list of 3 or more items
- Use real numbers and results whenever available
- Never start a post with "I"
- End with a question that invites the reader to engage
- No emojis unless the user explicitly requests them

## Content Pillars
1. **AI for GTM** — Claude Code, agents, automation workflows for sales and marketing
2. **Outbound** — Cold email, LinkedIn outreach, campaign building, personalization
3. **Paid Ads** — LinkedIn, Meta, Google — building and scaling campaigns with AI
4. **Frameworks & Playbooks** — Repeatable systems for GTM teams
5. **Tools & Stack** — Clay, Apollo, Instantly, Railway, N8N, and how they connect
