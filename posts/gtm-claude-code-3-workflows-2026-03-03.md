# Post: Claude Code for GTM — 3 Workflows
**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Source:** Coldiq Webinar — Kenny (Head of GTM) & Ivan (Head of Growth)
**Format:** List Post
**Hook Type:** Contrarian Statement

---

Claude Code is hyped for the right reasons.

Not because it writes code for you.

Because it lets non-technical GTM teams build autonomous workflows that used to require a full engineering sprint.

Here are 3 go-to-market flows you can deploy in your company today:

---

**1. End-to-End Outbound Campaign Builder**

- Upload a CSV of companies
- Claude Code tiers them into Tier 1/2/3 using your ICP scoring criteria (pure logic — no AI hallucinations)
- Finds the right contacts via Apollo's API
- Enriches missing emails through secondary providers
- Generates personalized sequences from your saved copy frameworks
- Pushes the full campaign live into Instantly

153 contacts → enriched → live campaign. ~20 minutes. No Clay. No copy-pasting. No tab-switching.

---

**2. LinkedIn Engagement → Outbound Pipeline**

- Scrapes everyone who likes or comments on your LinkedIn posts
- Runs ICP scoring + CRM de-duplication automatically
- Finds their work email via enrichment providers
- Routes qualified leads into an outbound sequence
- Runs weekly on autopilot — no one presses a button

Bonus: extend it to monitor competitor posts and keyword-based content. Anyone posting about your category enters your pipeline. One team replaced $300+/month in scraping tools with this single agent.

---

**3. Ad Campaign Builder Across LinkedIn, Meta & Google**

- Fill in a Google Sheet: campaign name, targeting, ad copy, landing page
- Run one prompt in Claude Code
- It calls the LinkedIn/Meta/Google Ads API automatically
- Pulls the right creatives from Google Drive
- Launches the campaign across platforms

A full campaign that used to take 30+ minutes in the UI now takes under 2 minutes — without touching a single dashboard.

---

The real unlock isn't any single workflow.

It's that your scoring criteria, copy frameworks, and past campaign data all live in one place — and compound every time you run a new campaign.
