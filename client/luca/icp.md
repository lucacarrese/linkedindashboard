ICP PROFILE — Luca Carrese / ColdIQ
Ideal Buyer
═══════════════════════════════════════

WHO THEY ARE:
B2B SaaS founders and revenue leaders at companies with an outbound motion — CEOs, COOs, CROs, VPs and Heads of Sales — who know their pipeline isn't scaling the way they need it to and are willing to rebuild the stack to fix it. They're not early in their AI curiosity; they're looking for working systems, not theory.

---

## TIER 1 — Primary Buyer (decision maker, fastest to close)

DEMOGRAPHICS:
- Role: CEO, CRO, COO, VP of Sales, Head of Sales
- Company: B2B SaaS, $1M–$30M ARR, 10–150 employees
- Industry: SaaS (any vertical), occasionally services with a sales team
- Team size: 3–20 person sales/GTM team
- Years in role: 2–8 years

THEIR BIGGEST PROBLEMS (in their words):
1. "Our outbound motion isn't producing consistent pipeline — it's too manual and too dependent on the rep."
2. "We're spending $20–50K/month on SDRs and the output doesn't justify it."
3. "We have a stack of tools (Clay, Apollo, Instantly, LinkedIn) but they're not connected — everything requires someone to babysit it."
4. "Our personalization doesn't scale. We either send garbage volume or high-quality emails that take hours per rep."
5. "We hired reps but they spend 60% of their time on list building and admin, not selling."

WHAT THEY'VE TRIED:
- Hiring more SDRs — didn't scale output, just scaled cost
- Building a basic Clay workflow — stopped at list building; didn't automate the rest of the pipeline
- Buying more tools (Apollo, ZoomInfo, Lusha) — added cost and data, didn't fix the workflow gap
- Trying AI copy tools — produced generic output that bombed reply rates

WHAT THEY ACTUALLY WANT:
- An outbound system that runs without daily supervision — lists built, emails sent, meetings booked
- Output quality that would take 10 SDRs to produce, run by 1–2 people
- Signal-based outreach — hitting prospects at the right moment, not spray-and-pray
- To understand what AI can actually do in their motion (not a demo — a working system)

BUYING TRIGGERS:
- Missed a quarterly pipeline target and the board is asking what's changing
- Just let go of 2–3 SDRs and need to maintain or increase output with fewer people
- Saw a competitor running an AI-powered outbound motion and is behind
- Just hired a GTM engineer or RevOps person and wants to give them a clear direction

OBJECTIONS:
- "We've tried automation before and it killed our deliverability." → Show signal-based sequencing — outreach based on intent, not volume
- "We don't have the engineering resources to build this." → Claude Code + ColdIQ system doesn't require engineering; it runs in Claude
- "Our sales cycle is complex — automation won't work." → Show account-based motion examples, not mass-blast

LANGUAGE MAP:
- DMs: "We're struggling to scale our outbound without just hiring more people."
- Sales calls: "We need to get more out of the team we have. The SDR model isn't working for us."
- Google: "AI outbound for B2B SaaS" / "Clay workflows for sales teams" / "outbound automation stack"
- Community: "Anyone using AI to replace their SDR motion?" (Slack groups, GTM communities)
- Internally: "How do we hit 2x pipeline with the same headcount?"

CONTENT THAT GRABS THEM:
- Posts that show a complete system (not a tool tip) — the whole workflow from signal to meeting booked
- Before/after with real time/cost/headcount numbers
- Named examples: "One team replaced $300+/month in scraping tools with this single agent"
- Anything with Clay, Instantly, Apollo, or Claude Code in the hook — they recognize the stack

NOT YOUR ICP:
- Enterprise (500+ employees) — too much process, too long a cycle
- Non-SaaS businesses without an outbound motion (e-commerce, marketplaces, consumer)
- Founders who want to learn AI in theory but won't implement
- RevOps practitioners without budget authority (they're Tier 2, not Tier 1)

## TIER 2 — Champion / Evaluator (influences the decision, longer path)

DEMOGRAPHICS:
- Role: GTM Engineer, RevOps Lead, Sales Ops Manager, Director of Demand Gen
- Company: Same as Tier 1 — $1M–$30M ARR B2B SaaS
- Team size: Reports to CRO or VP Sales; owns the tools stack
- Profile: Technically capable, has budget influence but not final authority

THEIR BIGGEST PROBLEMS (in their words):
1. "The leadership wants better pipeline but expects me to build the system — and I'm figuring it out as I go."
2. "We bought Clay and Instantly but the workflows are half-built. Nobody owns the sequencing logic."
3. "I'm rebuilding the outbound stack every 6 months because something always breaks."
4. "I need to show ROI on the tools budget before the next planning cycle."

WHAT THEY ACTUALLY WANT:
- A proven architecture they can implement — not just tool recs
- To be the person who brought the system in and made it work
- Templates, playbooks, specific prompts and flows they can adapt

CONTENT THAT GRABS THEM:
- Step-by-step workflow breakdowns (Pattern A posts)
- Tool stack posts with actual configurations
- Claude Code workflow posts — they want to build, not just read
- Posts that show the exact prompt or API call used

NOT YOUR ICP (Tier 2 version):
- Technical engineers with no GTM mandate — they'll geek out but never buy
- Ops people at companies where sales is fully inbound — outbound isn't their problem

---

## TIER 3 — Community / Practitioner (builds audience, rarely converts directly)

DEMOGRAPHICS:
- Role: AE, BDR/SDR, Account Manager, individual contributor in sales
- Company: Any size B2B company with a sales team
- Profile: AI-curious, early adopter, building their own workflows on the side

THEIR BIGGEST PROBLEMS:
1. "I spend half my time on admin that should be automated."
2. "My company isn't adopting AI fast enough — I'm building my own tools."
3. "I want to be the person who brings AI into the team."

CONTENT THAT GRABS THEM:
- Posts that validate their frustration ("Most teams do X manually — here's the agent that fixes it")
- Anything showing a single-rep workflow they could deploy themselves
- Lead magnets (templates, checklists, playbooks)

NOTE:
Tier 3 drives comments, reposts, and follower growth. They rarely convert to clients directly but build the social proof that attracts Tier 1 decision-makers.
