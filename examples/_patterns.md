# Post Structure Patterns
Extracted from 11 high-performing posts. Read this before drafting any post.

---

## Hook Formulas

Every hook contains a hard number. Always. No vague claims.

| Formula | Template | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Milestone + Promise** | "We just [specific result]. Here's [what reader gets]." | "We just crossed $6,000,000 ARR. Here's every sales tool we use." |
| **Volume Credibility + Promise** | "We [did X at massive scale] so you don't have to." | "We sent 1,000,000+ cold emails so you don't have to." |
| **Specific Result + Attribution** | "[Number] [outcome] at [company]. [Cost/time] to run." | "One GTM engineer just replaced 10 SDRs at Vercel. $1,000 a year to run." |
| **Stat + Pain** | "[X%] of [group] [positive thing]. But [contradicting reality]." | "Companies adopted AI agents. But most executives waste 15+ hours weekly." |
| **Objection Crusher** | "[Common excuse] should not be [valid] anymore." | "Not having a budget to run outbound should not be an excuse anymore." |
| **Subverted Expectation** | "[Normal-sounding thing] + [unexpected twist]." | "We just 'hired' our newest team member. No salary, no benefits, no coffee breaks." |
| **Personal Admission + Promise** | "I now [behavior shift]. Here are [N] things to know." | "I now spend more time in Claude Code than Clay." |

## Body Structure Patterns

### Pattern A — Workflow Teardown
*Use for: step-by-step systems, how something was built, processes*

```
[Problem or situation — 2-3 lines]

[Why old way failed — 1-2 isolated short lines]

[Transition: "So we built..." or "Here is the system:"]

1️⃣ [Step Name]
[1-2 line description]

2️⃣ [Step Name]
[1-2 line description]

Before: [old state]
After: [new state]

[2-3 short punchy closing lines — insight]
```

### Pattern B — Categorized Tool List
*Use for: tool roundups, resource lists, stacks by budget or function*

```
[Hook — problem + promise]

[Social proof or context — 1-2 lines]

[Transition: "Here is my list:" / "Here's my breakdown:"]

1️⃣ [Category Name]: [Tool names]
[1-2 line description of what it does]

2️⃣ [Category Name]: [Tool names]
[1-2 line description]

[Repeat for each category]

[2-line closing observation]

[CTA question] 👇
```

### Pattern C — Story + Attribution + Outcome
*Use for: case studies, third-party stories, real results from named sources*

```
[Specific result + company/person — 1-2 lines]

[Attribution: "Name (Title at Company) shared this on [source]."]

They started with:
→ [before state]
→ [before state]

[Transition: "Then [person/agent] stepped in."]

[2-3 lines of narrative — what changed]

Now [it/they]:
→ [capability]
→ [capability]

[Key insight with personal angle: "Key part for me as a [role]:"]

[Punchy 2-line observation]

The outcome:
→ [result]
→ [result]

[1-2 lines of reflection]

[CTA question]
```

### Pattern D — Numbered Education List
*Use for: tips, principles, things to know, how-to guides*

```
[Hook — personal admission or bold claim]

[Subhead: "Here are [N] things [audience] needs to know:"]

1. [Bold Title]
[2 sentence explanation. Specific, actionable.]

2. [Bold Title]
[2 sentence explanation.]

[Repeat for each point — no emojis on items]

[2-sentence closing wrap-up]

P.S: [CTA question]
```

## Formatting Rules

### The Arrow System
Used in every post. Three levels:

```
Category / Step Header
→ Primary item, tool, or action
   ↳ Description, context, or supporting detail
```

- `→` = the thing itself
- `↳` = why it matters or what it does
- Never use bullet points (`•`) for main lists — use `→`
- `•` only for pain-point lists (things going wrong)

### Line Break Rules
- Maximum 3 sentences per paragraph
- Every transition sentence gets its own isolated line
- "Before:" / "After:" always on their own lines
- Short punchy lines before the CTA always separated

### Numbers
- Always specific: $6,000,000 not "$6M" in hooks, 1,000,000+ not "over a million"
- Round numbers signal laziness — use the real figure
- At least one number in every hook

### Emoji Usage
- `1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣` — only when numbered steps are the main structure
- `👇` — only at the end of Type 3 CTAs (reflective close), never mid-post
- `🙌` — only in giveaway/celebration posts
- Maximum 2-3 emojis per post total
- **No emojis on individual list items in education-style posts**

### Bold
- Bold step titles when steps are complex (Pattern A, D)
- Never bold for decoration — only for navigation

## Closing Formula

Always one of three types:

**Type 1 — Contribution Question**
Invites peers to add to the list. Positions author as curator.
> "What [X] would you add?" / "What are some other tools that you use? 👇"

**Type 2 — Behavior Question**
Prompts self-reflection. Drives longer comment responses.
> "What is the first [X] you would [do]?" / "Have you been [doing X]?"

**Type 3 — Reflective Close + Question**
Two-step. An isolated insight line, then a question with 👇.
> "[Short declarative statement that reframes the post.]"
> ""
> "[Open question tied to reader's situation] 👇"

**P.S. Format** (variation of Type 2)
Used in education-style posts to soften the CTA.
> "P.S: [CTA question]"

## Voice Rules

- **Specific over vague.** Tool names always named (not "an enrichment tool" → "Prospeo.io")
- **Active verbs.** Built, deployed, converted, replaced, automated. No passive voice.
- **No hedging.** Never: "might," "could potentially," "it seems." State things as facts from direct experience.
- **"Just" signals recency.** "We just hired," "We just crossed" — makes achievements feel current
- **Sentence rhythm: short, short, medium.** Two short declaratives followed by one that expands.
- **Ownership is grounded.** "We" and "our" always come with a number or company signal ($6M ARR, 70+ clients, 48 agents)
- **Insider register.** Drop tool names without explanation. Reader either knows them or will look them up.

---

## What to Avoid

- Long paragraphs (more than 3 lines)
- Vague numbers ("millions," "many," "a lot")
- Starting key sentences with "I" (use "We," the tool name, or the outcome)
- Passive voice ("This was built by..." → "We built...")
- Hedging language ("might," "could," "potentially")
- Generic CTAs ("Let me know your thoughts" → ask a specific question)
- Dense blocks of text with no line breaks
