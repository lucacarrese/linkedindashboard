# Example 08 — How-To with Numbered Fixes
**Format:** Trend Observation → Pain Scenario → Numbered Fixes with → Sub-points + Examples
**Hook type:** Trend observation + pain (what a bad profile looks like)
**CTA type:** Niche-specific engagement question

---

Social presence is becoming more important than ever.

Especially when it comes to further converting cold email prospects.

Your social presence is a credibility check.

If a contact sees:
• Generic headline
• Old photo and banner
• Empty featured
• About section from 2019

They move on.

Here is how we fix this for outbound teams I work with:

1️⃣ Headline
Treat it like a mini landing page.
→ 3 keywords your ICP would search
→ Clear value statement
→ One result you helped deliver

Example:
"I help B2B SaaS teams book 3x more meetings | Cold email | Clay | Outbound systems"

2️⃣ About section (3 short paragraphs)
→ Paragraph 1: Who you help and what problem you solve
→ Paragraph 2: Proof it works (numbers, logos, quick wins)
→ Paragraph 3: Clear next step (DM, link, or calendar)

Make it easy for them to think "ok, this person can help me."

3️⃣ Featured section
Do not leave it empty.
Add:
→ One short case study
→ One lead magnet or guide
→ One post that shows your expertise

Featured is your storefront. Use it to prove your cold email claims.

4️⃣ Banner
One glance should tell them:
→ Who you help
→ What you help with
→ How to start

Tools like Taplio can audit your profile and benchmark you against top profiles in your niche in under 10 minutes. Helpful if you want a fast checklist of gaps.

You can have elite cold email copy.

If your LinkedIn profile does not back it up, you are leaving replies on the table.

Fix your profile first. Then send the emails.
